## Anthropic Expands Compute Deal with Google and Broadcom

Anthropic significantly expanded its compute agreement with Google and Broadcom this week, driven by surging demand for Claude models across enterprise customers. The company now serves over 1,000 business customers each spending more than $1 million annually. This comes on the heels of Anthropic's [$30 billion Series G funding round](https://www.cryptointegrat.com/p/ai-news-april-7-2026), which valued the company at approximately $380 billion -- making it one of the most valuable private companies in the world. The compute expansion signals that Anthropic is betting heavily on infrastructure to keep pace with enterprise adoption rather than relying solely on model improvements.

## Meta Unveils Muse Spark

Meta released [Muse Spark](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/8/muse-spark/), its first new model since Llama 4 launched nearly a year ago. Muse Spark is available via a private API preview on meta.ai, with a new bot-creation interface and on-device capabilities. Meta's benchmarks position it as competitive with Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and GPT 5.4 on selected tasks. The release also brings updates to the Telegram platform, with Meta-powered AI message editing that runs entirely on-device for privacy. Whether Muse Spark can challenge the current frontier models in real-world usage remains to be seen -- Meta's benchmark selections have historically been generous.

## Claude Code Gets Computer Use and MCP 500K

Anthropic shipped a major [Claude Code update](https://releasebot.io/updates/anthropic/claude-code) this week with several notable features. Computer use has landed in the CLI, meaning Claude can now open native applications, click through interfaces, test its own changes, and fix what breaks -- all from the terminal. The MCP (Model Context Protocol) tool result limit jumped to 500,000 characters, a significant increase that enables much richer tool interactions. Other additions include a Vertex AI setup wizard, improved sandbox safety, new Monitor tooling, an interactive learning system, and a batch of stability fixes across resume, permissions, terminal behavior, and voice mode. Claude Code also launched [Managed Agents](https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/release-notes/overview) in public beta -- a fully managed agent harness for running Claude autonomously with secure sandboxing and SSE streaming.

## Google Integrates NotebookLM into Gemini

Google [fully integrated NotebookLM](https://techbriefly.com/2026/04/10/google-fully-integrates-notebooklm-into-gemini/) into the Gemini app this week, allowing users to create and manage research notebooks directly within the chatbot interface. This merges Google's AI-powered research tool with its flagship model, creating a more unified experience for users who previously had to switch between apps. Separately, Google rolled out Gemini 3.1 Pro globally through the Gemini app, with enhanced reasoning capabilities for coding and data analysis. On the developer side, Google launched the Gemini Deep Research Agent in preview, which can autonomously plan, execute, and synthesize multi-step research tasks.

## Microsoft Ships Agent Framework 1.0

Microsoft released [Agent Framework 1.0](https://dev.to/alexmercedcoder/ai-tools-race-heats-up-week-of-april-3-9-2026-37fl), a production-ready unification of its Semantic Kernel and AutoGen frameworks. The release provides stable APIs, enterprise-grade multi-agent orchestration, and full MCP support for tool discovery and invocation. This is significant because it signals that multi-agent architectures are moving from experimental to production-ready. Companies that have been building custom orchestration layers now have a first-party Microsoft option with enterprise support. Alongside this, Microsoft announced a [$10 billion investment in Japan's AI infrastructure](https://www.cryptointegrat.com/p/ai-news-april-7-2026), continuing its aggressive global expansion of compute capacity.

## OpenAI Eyes IPO, Launches Safety Fellowship

OpenAI is on track to IPO as early as Q4 2026, with CEO Sam Altman and CFO Sarah Friar reportedly confident following the company's $122 billion fundraise. Enterprise revenue now makes up more than 40% of OpenAI's total and is on pace to reach parity with consumer revenue by year-end. On the safety front, OpenAI [launched the Safety Fellowship](https://www.humai.blog/ai-news-trends-april-2026-complete-monthly-digest/), a new program for external researchers to pursue AI safety and alignment work from September 2026 through February 2027. The company also published its Child Safety Blueprint, addressing child exploitation threats in the AI era. On the product side, ChatGPT Enterprise added GPT-5.3 Instant Mini as a fallback model and rolled out shared Outlook mailbox and calendar integration.

## Google Gemma 4 Goes Open Source

Google released [Gemma 4](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/), its latest open-source model family built from the same research as Gemini 3. Gemma 4 provides native support for function calling, structured JSON output, and system instructions -- features that make it practical for building autonomous agents. The model is positioned as the most capable open model on a byte-for-byte basis, directly competing with Meta's Llama series in the open-source arena. For developers who need function-calling capabilities without paying for API access, Gemma 4 is worth evaluating.
